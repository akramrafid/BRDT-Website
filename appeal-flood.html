Bangladesh Flood Crisis

Bangladesh is a low-lying delta nation criss-crossed by over 700 rivers. Every year, seasonal monsoons inundate vast swathes of the country — displacing millions of people, destroying homes, crops, and livelihoods in rural communities like Belghar.

The Belghar region in Bangladesh is particularly vulnerable to flooding. When floodwaters rise, the rural poor suffer the most — their mud homes collapse, their food stores are destroyed, and they are left without safe drinking water, shelter, or income for weeks at a time.

The situation on the ground: During a severe flood season, entire villages in Belghar become inaccessible by road. Families are stranded for days with nothing to eat. BRDT's rapid-response team mobilises within 24–48 hours to reach the most affected families with emergency relief packages.

What Your Donation Provides

  • Emergency food parcels: rice, lentils, oil, and dried foods
  • Clean drinking water and water purification tablets
  • Warm clothing, blankets and essential household items
  • Temporary shelter materials for displaced families
  • Cash assistance for families to rebuild their livelihoods

BRDT's flood relief programme is built around speed, dignity, and community. Our local volunteers know the terrain and the people, ensuring aid reaches every affected family — including the elderly, disabled, and those without anyone to help them.

How Your Donation Helps

BRDT's proven flood relief process ensures your donation reaches those in crisis quickly and effectively.

Rapid Response

When floods hit, every hour matters. BRDT's local volunteer network mobilises within 24–48 hours to assess affected areas and begin distributing emergency food, water, and essential supplies to the most vulnerable families in Belghar.

Emergency Relief Packs

Your donation funds emergency relief packages containing dry food rations, clean drinking water, basic medicines, and warm blankets. Each pack is carefully assembled to sustain a family of five for up to two weeks during the flood crisis.

Rebuild & Recover

After the immediate crisis, BRDT supports families through recovery — providing cash assistance to rebuild collapsed homes, replace lost livestock, and restore agricultural livelihoods so families can stand on their feet again with dignity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find out how BRDT responds to flood disasters in Bangladesh.

How quickly does BRDT respond to a flood?

BRDT has a dedicated network of local volunteers in the Belghar area who are trained for rapid disaster response. Within 24 to 48 hours of a flood event being confirmed, our team is on the ground assessing damage and distributing emergency supplies to affected families.

Will my donation only help if there is an active flood?

Flood relief donations go into a dedicated emergency fund. When there is no active flood emergency, funds are held in reserve for the next disaster. Bangladesh experiences flooding almost every year, so your donation will always be used to help flood survivors. Unspent funds may also be redirected to general disaster relief.

How does BRDT reach isolated villages during floods?

BRDT's volunteer team uses boats and local knowledge to reach villages cut off by floodwaters. We prioritise communities that are most isolated and most vulnerable — particularly those with elderly residents, young children, or people with disabilities who cannot self-evacuate.

Can I donate for a specific type of flood relief?

Yes. When donating, you can specify whether your funds should go to emergency food, clean water, shelter, or general flood relief. BRDT honours donor specifications and ensures your money funds exactly what you intend.

Standing With Bangladesh in Crisis

Every year, BRDT stands with the people of Belghar when floodwaters rise. Thanks to generous donors, we have delivered emergency relief to thousands of families — ensuring they have food, clean water, and hope when everything else has been swept away.
