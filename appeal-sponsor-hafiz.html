What is Sponsoring a Hafiz?

A Hafiz (plural: Huffaz) is a person who has memorised the entire Holy Quran — all 6,236 verses — by heart. This is one of the most noble and revered achievements in Islam. At BRDT's Madrasah in Belghar, dedicated students spend years in rigorous study to achieve this sacred goal.

Many of these students come from poor rural families who cannot afford the basic necessities required to sustain their studies — food, accommodation, learning materials, and clothing. By sponsoring a Hafiz student, you directly remove financial barriers that would otherwise prevent them from completing this sacred journey.

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said: "The best of you are those who learn the Quran and teach it." — Sahih Bukhari. By sponsoring a Hafiz, you become part of this chain of knowledge — earning reward every time they recite, teach, or lead prayers with the Quran you helped them memorise.

What Your Sponsorship Covers

  • Daily meals and nutritional support for the student
  • Accommodation at BRDT Madrasah dormitory
  • Quran textbooks, notebooks, and learning materials
  • Clothing and essential personal items
  • Qualified teacher salaries and lesson planning

When your sponsored student completes their Hifz (memorisation) and becomes a Hafiz, they will lead prayers in mosques, teach the next generation, and preserve Allah's word — and you will share in every reward earned from their contribution, long after your time in this world.

The Reward of Sponsoring a Hafiz

One of the most spiritually rewarding acts of Sadaqah Jariyah you can undertake.

Preserve Allah's Word

Every Hafiz who memorises the Quran is a living guardian of Allah's word. By sponsoring their journey, you become an instrument of Quran preservation — earning reward every time the student recites, teaches, or leads prayers for the rest of their life.

Intercession on Qiyamah

The Prophet ﷺ taught that on the Day of Judgement, the Hafiz will be dressed in a crown of honour and their parents will be given a light that outshines the sun. As a sponsor, you share in supporting this noble journey and its eternal rewards.

Raise Islamic Scholars

Today's Hafiz students become tomorrow's Imams, teachers, and Islamic scholars. Your sponsorship doesn't just help one student — it ripples through generations as they go on to teach others, lead communities, and inspire future Huffaz across Bangladesh.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about sponsoring a Hafiz student at BRDT.

How long does it take a student to become a Hafiz?

On average, it takes 3 to 5 years to complete the Hifz programme, depending on the student's age, dedication, and prior Quranic knowledge. BRDT Madrasah provides a structured, supportive environment to help students progress steadily under the guidance of qualified teachers.

Will I receive updates about my sponsored student?

Yes. BRDT provides periodic progress updates on the Madrasah programme, including milestones achieved by Hafiz students. We protect student privacy, but sponsors receive reports on the programme's progress and the impact of their donations.

Can I sponsor a Hafiz in memory of someone?

Absolutely. Many donors sponsor a Hafiz in memory of a deceased parent, spouse, or loved one. The reward of every Quranic recitation that the student makes is gifted to the person in whose name you donate — a beautiful and enduring act of Sadaqah Jariyah.

Is sponsoring a Hafiz considered Sadaqah Jariyah?

Yes — sponsoring a Hafiz student is one of the most powerful forms of Sadaqah Jariyah. It falls under the category of "beneficial knowledge" mentioned in the famous hadith: "When a person dies, their deeds end except for three: continuing charity, beneficial knowledge, or a righteous child who prays for them."

Guardians of the Quran in Bangladesh

BRDT Madrasah in Belghar has produced dozens of Huffaz who now lead prayers, teach the Quran, and serve as pillars of their communities. Every one of them was supported by generous donors who believed in the power of Islamic education.
