Our Ramadan Projects

Discover the humanitarian initiatives we lead to empower and uplift rural communities during the holy month.

Initiative 01

Provides mentoring and overall support to students

Belghar Rural Development Trust (BRDT) plays a vital role in strengthening the educational foundation of students from underprivileged communities. The organization provides structured mentoring and ongoing support to young learners in both reading and writing, which helps build confidence and skills.

Through individualized guidance, students receive mentoring sessions to meet their individual learning needs. This approach ensures that they do not fall behind, but are encouraged to progress at their own pace. The Trust's mentors often act not only as teachers but also as role models, creating a positive learning environment.

Overall, BRDT provides essential books, notebooks, pens, calculators, geometry boxes and shoulder bags to students from Kinder Garden to Class V, Class VI to Secondary School Certificate (SSC), From college to university level. We also provide financial assistance to those who need money to fill up forms.

Initiative 02

Distribution of essential warm clothes and blankets to underprivileged people during winter

BRDT plays a compassionate role in helping the needy during the winter months. Realizing the difficulties faced by underprivileged families, the organization organizes regular distribution programs to provide essential warm clothes and blankets to the families who can't effort to buy those things.

As the temperature drops, many individuals - especially those living in rural and economically backward villages – struggle to protect themselves from the cold. The trust then steps in by collecting and distributing sweaters, jackets, shawls and blankets to the needy. These efforts not only provide physical warmth, but also provide a sense of care and dignity to the recipients.

Volunteers and village members actively participate in these initiatives, identifying the neediest families and helping to ensure that aid reaches them efficiently. These distribution programs bring the people of the village together, which fosters a sense of solidarity and collective responsibility.

Initiative 03

Providing Sehri and Iftar items for needy families at the beginning of Ramadan

Belghar Rural Development Trust carries out meaningful humanitarian efforts by organizing Sehri and Iftar for needy families during the holy month of Ramadan. Recognizing the spiritual and physical importance of these meals, the Trust ensures that underprivileged families can observe the fast with dignity and comfort.

At the beginning of Ramadan, we distribute essential food items. These initiatives are especially important for families who may otherwise struggle to procure nutritious food during this holy month. Our volunteers work tirelessly to identify the needy early and coordinate timely food deliveries.

This effort not only prevents hunger, but also instils a sense of inclusion and social consciousness, which helps families fully participate in the traditions of Ramadan. By supporting needy families during Ramadan, the organization reinforces the values of compassion, sharing, and unity.

Initiative 04

Providing Semai & sugar for the poor on the day of Eid ul-Fitr

The organization provides assistance to the underprivileged community by distributing semai, sugar and spice to them during the joyous festival of Eid ul-Fitr. These essential ingredients are traditionally used to prepare festive sweets, which help families celebrate the day with joy and a sense of cultural unity.

At the end of Ramadan, many low-income families struggle to afford even the simplest festive food. Realizing this, we organize timely distribution activities so that no family is left out of this celebration. By providing semai and sugar, we enable families to prepare their traditional meals and share in the joy of Eid.

By helping families celebrate Eid with traditional foods, the organization strengthens social bonds and spreads the true spirit of generosity and unity in joy and sorrow.

Initiative 05

Delivering sacrificial meat to the homes of those who are unable to sacrifice on Eid al-Adha

Belghar Rural Development Trust has taken up another humanitarian initiative on the occasion of Eid ul-Adha. Through this initiative, sacrificial meat is delivered to the homes of families who are unable to perform the sacrifice themselves. This effort reflects the true spirit of sharing and generosity, which is the essence of this festival.

On Eid ul-Adha, many needy families cannot afford to sacrifice a cow or a goat due to financial constraints. The trust bridges the gap between the rich and the poor by either sacrificing or procuring meat from others and distributing a portion of the meat to the needy. Volunteers manage this process with utmost care.

This initiative not only provides nutritious food, but also gives underprivileged families an opportunity to participate in the joy and tradition of the festival. Consuming sacrificial meat brings a sense of belonging and dignity among them and reminds them that they are valued members of society.

Initiative 06

Providing timely assistance to people in need in various disasters and emergency situations

The Belghar Rural Development Trust plays a vital role in supporting vulnerable communities by providing timely assistance to those in need during various disasters. Whether it is a flood, storm or other unforeseen crisis, the organization responds quickly to alleviate suffering and help families bounce back.

During disasters, many rural families are deprived of food, shelter and basic necessities. The Trust then steps in to distribute emergency relief items such as dry food, clean drinking water, clothing and temporary shelter and money. Their swift action ensures that affected families receive help when they need it most.

Volunteers and local supporters work together to identify the most affected areas and ensure equitable distribution of aid. Beyond immediate relief, the organization often provides assistance through rehabilitation efforts, which help families rebuild their lives and regain a sense of stability.

Initiative 07

Planting trees and fruit-bearing plants along roadsides or in vacant areas with proper permission

Belghar Rural Development Trust actively works towards environmental sustainability and community well-being by planting trees on unused land or roadsides in the village with the permission of the landowners. This initiative reflects a well-thought-out approach to both environmental conservation and social benefits.

By using vacant land and roadside areas, the trust transforms unused spaces into green areas that contribute to creating a healthy environment. Planting fruit trees not only enhances natural beauty, but also helps improve air quality and support local biodiversity.

Importantly, these trees become a collective asset for the community. When the trees grow and bear fruit, they provide readily available nutrition for the villagers, especially those who cannot afford to plant their own trees.

Initiative 08

Providing assistance in the marriage of a daughter from a poor family

Provides compassionate support to the economically backward community by assisting the marriage of daughters from helpless poor families. Realizing the financial burden that marriage can place on such families, the organization steps in to ease their hardships and ensure that this important event of life can be completed with dignity.

The trust provides various forms of assistance, including essential household items, clothing and financial assistance to meet the initial expenses of the wedding. This assistance not only relieves the families from immense emotional stress, but also enables them to celebrate the occasion with dignity and joy.

Volunteers and community members often collaborate to identify the genuine needy families, so that the aid reaches the most deserving. By helping daughters start a new chapter in life with dignity, the organization reinforces the values of care, respect and collective support.

Our Expanding Mission

More Initiatives, More Impact

Beyond our core projects, BRDT leads a growing portfolio of community programmes — each one designed to uplift, empower and sustain rural Bangladesh.

Initiative 09

Healthy Environment — Cleanliness & Environmental Protection

Belghar Rural Development Trust plays a vital role in cleanliness and environmental protection in the community. With a strong commitment to creating a healthy and sustainable environment, the organisation actively participates in various initiatives to raise awareness and encourage responsible habits among villagers.

The Trust organises cleanliness drives, which motivate residents to keep their surroundings free from waste and pollution. These activities often include cleaning public places, roads and water sources, which helps improve sanitation and prevent the spread of diseases. By involving local volunteers, the organisation instils a sense of pride in maintaining a clean environment.

In addition to cleanliness efforts, the Trust also promotes environmental protection through tree plantation, waste management awareness and education on sustainable living practices. These initiatives help conserve natural resources and encourage the community to adopt eco-friendly habits in their daily lives.

Initiative 10

Healthcare for Poor — Medical Expenses & Free Medical Camps

We strive to play a vital role in improving access to healthcare for people in remote and underprivileged villages. Through dedicated initiatives, the Trust ensures that no one is deprived of basic medical care due to financial constraints or lack of facilities.

One of the Trust's major contributions is providing timely medical care to the poor and helpless villagers. Many rural families struggle to reach hospitals or afford consultations — the Trust bridges this gap by providing access to doctors and essential healthcare services at low or no cost.

The Trust also supports patients by covering or reducing the cost of medicines, particularly important for those suffering from chronic diseases or requiring long-term treatment.

A major aspect of the Trust's work is organising free medical camps in the village. These camps bring together qualified doctors and healthcare volunteers who provide general health check-ups, primary diagnostic services and health advice. Medicines are often distributed on the spot, ensuring immediate relief for patients who might otherwise have been denied treatment.

Initiative 11

Skills for Life — Preparing Disadvantaged Youth for Employment

BRDT has become a powerful force for change in communities where young people often struggle with limited opportunities, social barriers and a lack of guidance. The Trust steps in with compassion, discipline and practical training to transform "problem youth" into productive, employable individuals.

The Trust's core mission is the belief that every young person can become employable, given the right environment. Through structured vocational programmes, life skills training and hands-on learning, participants build confidence and skills across technical trades, agriculture, tailoring, computer literacy and other job-based fields.

In addition to technical training, the Trust emphasises personal development — teamwork, communication, responsibility and problem-solving. Mentors and trainers work closely with each young person, helping them replace negative habits with productive routines and a new sense of purpose.

Young people who once felt lost now emerge with employable skills, self-esteem and a clear direction for the future. Many find stable jobs, start small businesses, or become role models in their communities.

Initiative 12

Empowering Community — Reducing Unemployment & Alleviating Poverty

BRDT has emerged as a cornerstone of progress in communities where unemployment and poverty have long hindered social and economic growth. With a clear mission and participatory approach, the Trust undertakes small and large-scale development projects to improve quality of life and strengthen entire villages.

At the heart of its work is a commitment to creating sustainable livelihoods. The Trust identifies local needs, mobilises resources and implements projects that create employment — from skill development and small enterprise support to agricultural development and community infrastructure. Each project is tailored to the strengths of the region, ensuring long-term impact.

Small projects focus on empowering individuals and families through vocational training, self-help groups, cottage industries and youth programmes. Larger projects address irrigation systems, rural connectivity and market-linked production units.

By combining strategic planning with grassroots engagement, BRDT addresses the root causes of poverty. The result is a growing network of confident, skilled and economically active individuals who contribute to the overall development of their communities.

Initiative 13

Community First, Always — Progress Through Partnership

BRDT has established itself as a reliable pillar in the journey of social development. People come to BRDT whenever they face a challenge. Where communities face limited resources, lack of awareness and barriers to essential services, the Trust steps in with guidance, coordination and practical support.

Whether improving education, supporting healthcare, strengthening livelihoods or promoting community well-being, the Trust plays an active role in every aspect of social development. Its team works closely with local groups, government agencies and partner organisations to identify urgent needs and implement lasting solutions.

From organising awareness programmes and skill-building workshops to assisting in infrastructure development and supporting vulnerable families, BRDT ensures aid reaches those who need it most. Its approach is rooted in collaboration and empowerment — encouraging communities to participate, take responsibility and build resilience.

What makes BRDT truly unique is its readiness to support any task that contributes to social progress. Through its consistent efforts, BRDT continues to strengthen communities, reduce inequalities and pave the way for a more inclusive and hopeful future.

Initiative 14

Establish Education & Cultural Centres — Knowledge Meets Heritage

Belghar Rural Development Trust has long recognised that true social progress begins with knowledge, creativity and cultural pride. The Trust actively establishes, manages and supports educational institutions and cultural centres that serve as pillars of progress for rural communities.

The Trust works to create accessible learning environments where children, youth and adults can acquire the skills and confidence they need to shape their futures. By supporting colleges, schools, madrasahs, maktabs, training centres and literacy programmes, it ensures education reaches the most disadvantaged areas. These institutions provide not only formal education but also values, discipline and personal development.

Along with education, the Trust attaches great importance to the preservation and promotion of cultural heritage. It encourages the development of traditional local customs through cultural centres, community halls and art initiatives — hubs of creativity, identity and social bonding.

By combining educational advancement with cultural enrichment, BRDT plays a vital role in building a conscious, confident and culturally rich society whose knowledge and tradition remain a strong foundation for future generations.

Initiative 15

Manage Madrasah — Belghar Madinatul Ulum Madrasah & Orphanage

Belghar Rural Development Trust is playing a vital role in shaping the future of underprivileged children by renovating and running Belghar Madinatul Ulum Madrasah and Orphanage — providing a modern, safe and well-organised environment where children receive both academic and moral education.

The Madrasah imparts quality education rooted in cultural and spiritual values, ensuring students grow up with strong character, discipline and a sense of purpose. The orphanage, run with the same compassionate approach, provides shelter, food, healthcare and psychological support to children who have lost their parents.

The Trust ensures every child is treated with dignity and given the opportunity to learn, grow and dream. Beyond basic needs, the organisation focuses on holistic development — encouraging participation in extra-curricular activities, sports, competitive skill-building programmes and social engagement.

Through its dedication in running Belghar Madinatul Ulum Madrasah and Orphanage, BRDT continues to transform the lives of children, strengthen the social fabric of the entire upazila and install hope and opportunity where it is needed most.
